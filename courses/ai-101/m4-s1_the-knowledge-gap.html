Module 4

The Knowledge Gap

Every AI tool you've used so far knows nothing about your business. Your products, your clients, your processes, your pricing — none of it. This section explains why, and what you can do about it.

Every turn is a full re-read

As discussed earlier: every time you send a message, the model re-reads the entire conversation from scratch. Your first message, its reply, your second message, its reply — all of it, fed back in, every single time.

There's no accumulating understanding. No relationship building over a conversation. Each response is a fresh prediction based on a full re-read. The impression of a flowing conversation is an illusion created by the model seeing the transcript.

Practically: instructions from early in a long thread carry less weight than something you just said. Important context should be restated, not assumed. If the AI starts ignoring what you told it twenty messages ago, this is why.

Close the chat, the context clears

Close the chat, the context window clears. Next session starts empty.

The platforms are starting to fix the basics. Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini now have cross-session memory — the AI remembers facts about you between conversations. Your name, your role, your preferences. But it's a summary layer, not deep knowledge. Basic and improving fast.

The bigger gap: AI doesn't know your business. Your products, your policies, your clients, your processes. That's not a memory problem — that's a customisation problem. And there's a spectrum of ways to solve it, from free and instant to expensive and slow.

Module 1

Everyone's Talking, Nobody's Doing

There's a lot of noise around AI right now. Depending on who you listen to, it's either about to replace half the workforce or solve every problem your business has ever had. Neither of those is particularly useful when you're just trying to get through the week.

This course is about cutting through that. By the end you'll have a clear picture of what's actually going on, what matters for your context, and what you can safely ignore.

What are you hearing?

There are two stories running right now, and most of what you read falls into one of them.

The middle

AI is going to replace us

Jobs are disappearing, entire teams are being restructured, and if you're not already using it you're behind.

AI is a magic solution

Just subscribe, just plug it in, just click the button and watch everything get better.

Both contain some truth, which is what makes them tricky. Lean too far into the first and you either freeze or spend money out of panic. Lean into the second and you buy tools you don't understand from people selling confidence.

Most businesses are somewhere in the middle — aware something is shifting, not yet sure what to do about it. That's a reasonable place to be.

What's real?

The shifts everyone talks about in big tech are already showing up closer to home. Accounting firms, trades businesses, salons, property management companies — anywhere people do repetitive work with digital tools, something is changing.

A sole trader can now produce marketing that looks like it came from an agency. A three-person accounting firm can process work that used to need ten. A local manufacturer can forecast demand using the same AI that powers global supply chains.

The question isn't whether AI is relevant to your business. It's which parts of your business it's already relevant to — and whether you've noticed yet.

Module 2

Types of AI

Not all AI does the same thing. That sounds obvious but it's the mistake most businesses make first. Someone sees a competitor using AI and assumes they need the same thing. A vendor pitches "AI-powered" and nobody asks what that actually means.

Picking the wrong type of AI for the wrong problem is like hiring an accountant to fix your plumbing. Both are skilled. One is useless for the job.

This module gives you a practical map. There's a lot of terminology flying around and most of it falls into three questions: How capable is it? How does it work? What does it actually do?

The third one is where you'll spend most of your time — that's where the decisions live.

How capable is it?

Three levels. Only one exists.

ANI
Narrow AI
One job, does it well. Everything on the market today.
AGI
General AI
Human-level reasoning across any domain. Doesn't exist yet.
ASI
Super AI
Surpasses humans at everything. Also doesn't exist yet.

When anyone says "AI" they mean narrow. The gap between narrow and general is enormous. Worth knowing these terms so you can spot when someone's selling a vision instead of a product.

How does it work?

Before we get into the types you'd actually choose between, a quick look at what's happening underneath. You don't need to remember any of this — it's here so the terminology doesn't trip you up when a vendor or a headline throws it at you.

Not all AI works the same way. Some follows rules someone wrote. Some learns from data. The difference matters because it changes what a tool can and can't do for you.

Rule-based systems
If/then logic. No learning. Predictable, rigid. Still powers a lot of business software.
Machine learning (ML)
Finds its own patterns from data. The more data, the better it gets. Behind most "smart" features in your tools.
Deep learning
ML with many layers. The breakthrough that made modern AI useful. Powers image recognition, language models, voice.
Reinforcement learning
Learns by trial and error. Reward what works, penalise what doesn't. How robotics and game-playing AI improve.
Neuro-symbolic AI
Combines pattern recognition with logical reasoning. The emerging approach for 2026. AI that can explain why it reached an answer, not just give you one.

What does it actually do?

This is what most people mean when they talk about "types of AI." Not the method — the job it does, the problem it solves. These are the types you'd choose between when looking at a real business problem.
